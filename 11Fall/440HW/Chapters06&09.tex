Recall: When it's a disproof, you get an addition 2 bonus points for stating a true statement and proving it.\\

\begin{enumerate}

\item Prove or disprove: If $g_1H=g_2H$, then $Hg_1=Hg_2$.
\item Prove or disprove: $(\mathbb{R},+)$ is isomorphic to $(\mathbb{R}^\times, \cdot)$.
\item Let $I_{\mathbb{R}^\times} = \left\{\begin{pmatrix}r & 0 \\ 0 & r\end{pmatrix}\mid r \in \mathbb{R}^\times\right\}$. Then $I_{\mathbb{R}^\times} \subseteq \text{GL}_2(\mathbb{R})$ and the set of right cosets $$I_{\mathbb{R}^\times} \text{GL}_2(\mathbb{R}) = \{I_{\mathbb{R}^\times}M \mid M \in \text{GL}_2(\mathbb{R})\}$$ forms a group under matrix multiplication. Let the linear transformations on $\mathbb{C}$ be denoted $$L=\left\{f(x)=\dfrac{ax+b}{cx+d} \mid a,b,c,d \in \mathbb{R}, ad-bc \neq 0\right\}.$$ Then $L$ is a group under functional composition. Prove: $I_{\mathbb{R}^\times} \text{GL}_2(\mathbb{R}) \cong L$.

\end{enumerate}
