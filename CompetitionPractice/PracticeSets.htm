About the competitions:

MAA Team Contest: Students work together in groups of up to 3 students on a set of 10 problems. The contest is administered on the WSU campus during Saturday morning. Coffee, juice and donuts will be supplied.
Difficulty: Not too bad, past WSU teams usually got at least 3 or 4 questions right. Our best teams in years past have placed in the top 10 in the state. You can do it!

 

Putnam: The William Lowell Putnam Competition is an annual mathematics competition for undergraduate students in the United States and Canada that has been held since 1938. (The overall winner gets a scholarship for Harvard graduate school.) Students work alone on 2 sessions of problems, each with 6 questions. Sessions are 3 hours long and separated by a lunch break (9am-noon and 2-5pm). The competition is administered on the WSU campus. Coffee, juice and donuts will be supplied.
Difficulty: Insanely hard, BUT if you can get even one answer right (which plenty of WSU students in the past have achieved), graduate schools will take notice. Just try it! Also, the more often you take it, the better you get. So freshman and sophomore should NOT shy away.
