Desire2Learn: Course materials can be found on Desire2Learn (D2L) including approximate grades. If you ever want an exact look at your grade, email the instructor directly.

Google and Desmos: The course will frequently use Google Sheets. You will need to create a (free) Google account (or use an existing one you have) to work in Google Sheets. We will also be using the graphing calculator at Desmos.com. When setting-up your Desmos account, it is strongly recommended to sign up with your Google account.

Prerequisite: MATH050 or placement exam

About This Course: Applications of rates and linear functions, modeling with spreadsheets and graphing calculators, optimization/linear programming, and the mathematics of business and finance.

D2L Progress: In this online course, you will be expected to complete the material provided in D2L. If at any point you are uncertain what is expected of you on a given day, please email me. For just interacting with D2L and working through the sample problems, watching the videos, etc. you earn the 90 points for Content Interaction. This is measured in the Progress part of D2L. Also, make sure you mark the Reading Assignments in D2L as Completed once you have done them.

Exit Problems: At the end of most topics there is an Exit Problem that you should complete and submit to the assignments folder in D2L. Feedback on your submission will (hopefully) be posted within 1 business day. If there are mistakes or errors in your submission, you can resubmit (as many times as needed and as time allows). As soon as you complete the exit problem correctly, you will receive credit for it. There is no partial credit for Exit Problems.

WebAssign Homework: Some topics have an associated set of homework problems assigned through the WebAssign website. Use the link in D2L for the first time you access WebAssign (this will ensure you are connected to the correct course). Afterwards, you can access WebAssign through D2L or via webassign.net. Suggested due dates for WebAssign homework can be found on D2L; firm deadlines for the homework is given in WebAssign. WebAssign problems can be resubmitted as many times as needed before the due date. (Note: You do NOT have to mark WebAssign assignments as complete in D2L. Whether you do or not, Im going to just pull all scores in from WebAssign periodically.)

Quizzes: Each module will end with a Quiz based on the Exit Problems and WebAssign homework from that module. There will be 6 Quizzes in all (see schedule below). Each Quiz will count for 15 points. You only have 1 attempt for Quizzes and partial credit is possible. All Quizzes for a unit must be completed before the corresponding Unit Exam.

Unit Exams and Final: There will be three Unit Exams and one comprehensive Final Exam. See schedule below.

Academic Dishonesty: Any type of academic dishonesty will result in failure and will be reported to school authorities. This includes:

         student-to-student communication during a quiz or exam,

         using unsanctioned homework help websites to answer homework/quiz/exam problems, and/or

         using Artificial Intelligence/Large Language Models to answer homework/quiz/exam problems.

If you are having trouble with an assignment, please contact the instructor.

Note: This syllabus is subject to change if deemed necessary by the instructor.
