D2L Progress:            In this online course, you will be expected to complete the material provided in D2L. If at any point you are uncertain what is expected of you on a given day, please email me. For just interacting with D2L and working through the sample problems, watching the videos, etc. you earn the 75 points for Content Interaction. This is measured in the “Progress” part of D2L. Also, make sure you mark the Reading Assignments in D2L as “Completed” once you have done them.

Exit Problems:           After working through the individual problems and checking your understanding against the posted solution, most sections contain an exit problem that you should complete before taking the quiz. The exit problem closely matches the type of questions that will be asked on the next quiz. As soon as you complete the exit problem correctly, you will receive credit for it. There is no partial credit for Exit Problems. If there are mistakes or errors in your submission, feedback will be given in D2L and you can resubmit (as many times as needed). You should not move on in the course until you have completed the exit problem.

WebAssign Homework:        Some sections will have homework problems assigned through the WebAssign website. You can access this through D2L. Due dates for WebAssign homework can be found in D2L Assignments.

Quizzes:   There will be 7 quizzes (see schedule below). Each quiz will count for 15 points. Quiz problems are based on the individual problems and exit problems done in the preceding material. Quizzes completed before the due date will be returned with feedback within a week (or before an upcoming exam). Quizzes completed after the due date will be accepted, but the feedback from the instructor may be delayed. Quizzes cannot be accessed until the corresponding Checklists are marked as completed.

Unit Exams and Final:          There will be 3 unit exams and one comprehensive final exam. Unit exams cannot be accessed until the corresponding quizzes have been completed.

Instructor Feedback:            Exit problems, quizzes, and exams submitted through D2L will receive feedback in D2L. If something has been marked incorrect and the feedback for why is missing or incomplete, do not hesitate to contact the instructor.

Late/Missed Work: In the asynchronous format, some deadlines are firm and some are flexible. In general, Checklist completion must be completed on time to earn any credit. Exit problems, WebAssign, and Quizzes have suggested due dates to keep you on track in the course, but there is no penalty for submission after the suggest due date. However, one consequence of completing an Exit problem, WebAssign section, or Quiz past the due date is that instructor feedback may be delayed. Unit Exams must be completed by the scheduled due date, but you are allowed to ask for an extension, and one will be granted at the discretion of the instructor. The due date for the Final Exam (Noon on May 8, 2025) is firm.

Academic Dishonesty:  Any type of academic dishonesty (cheating, copying, plagiarism, using a solutions manual to do homework, etc.) will result in failure and will be reported to school authorities. This includes student-to-student communication during a quiz or exam and/or using unsanctioned “homework help” and/or AI websites to answer homework/quiz/exam problems. If you are having trouble with an assignment, please see the instructor first.

Note:   This syllabus is subject to change if deemed necessary by the instructor.
