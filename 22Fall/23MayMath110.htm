D2L Progress:           In this online course, you will be expected to complete the material provided in D2L. The D2L “Class Progress” link provides an overview of what you have completed. If at any point you are uncertain why you are missing progress, please email me and I will let you know what you are missing and/or how to make it up.

Exit Problems:          At the end of most topics there is an Exit Problem that you should complete and submit to the assignments folder in D2L. Feedback on your submission will (hopefully) be posted within 1 business day. If there are mistakes or errors in your submission, you can resubmit (as many times as needed). As soon as you complete the exit problem correctly, you will receive credit for it. There is no partial credit for Exit Problems.

WebAssign Homework:       Some topics have an associated set of homework problems assigned through the WebAssign website. You can access this through D2L or via webassign.net. Suggested due dates for WebAssign homework can be found in WebAssign and on D2L. WebAssign problems can be resubmitted as many times as needed before the due date.

Short Challenges:     Each module will end with a Quiz based on the Exit Problems from that module. There will be 12 Quizzes in all (see schedule below). Each Quiz will count for 15 points and the lowest Quiz score will be dropped from your grade. You only have 1 attempt for Quizzes and partial credit is possible. All Quizzes for a unit must be completed before the corresponding Unit Exam.

Unit Exams and Final:         There will be three Unit Exams and one comprehensive Final Exam. Exam dates are tentative until officially announced in class. The final exam is tentatively scheduled for Monday, December 8, 1:00 – 3:00pm. Occasionally performance on an exam may replace a score on a challenge.

Academic Dishonesty:  Any type of academic dishonesty (cheating, copying, plagiarism, using a solutions manual to do homework, etc.) will result in failure and will be reported to school authorities. This includes student-to-student communication during a challenge or exam and/or using unsanctioned “homework help” websites to answer homework/challenge/exam problems. If you are having trouble with an assignment, please see the instructor first.

Note:   This syllabus is subject to change if deemed necessary by the instructor.
